The Classroom Web

Inferential connections between student strategies in a single subtraction lesson

In a classroom of 24 students, there are 276 possible pairwise connections on a single topic. No teacher can track all of them. This visualization makes the inferential connections between student strategies explicit — showing that mathematical understanding is not a linear conversation but a web of interdependent ideas.

Four students solve 83 − 69. Each arrives at the answer differently: base-ten blocks, a 120 chart, symbolic equations, compensation. The edges between them are not just "similarities" — they are questions a teacher might pose to help one student recognize their thinking in another's work. The gold star is the lesson goal: the dashed lines show how each strategy advances toward it, with thicker lines marking strategies that carry more of the goal's weight.

Click a student node to highlight its connections. Hover over an edge to read the linking question. Drag to rearrange.
